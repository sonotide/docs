---
title: "Migration Guide"
description: "A step-by-step path for elegantly migrating an existing raw WASAPI codebase to Sonotide."
---

If your application already has a working but complex WASAPI backend, ripping it all out at once is a recipe for regressions. The safest way to introduce Sonotide is to replace the reusable audio layers step-by-step, leaving your product-specific business logic untouched until the new path is perfectly stable.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Step 1: Move Device Enumeration">
    Replace direct `IMMDeviceEnumerator` usage with `runtime::enumerate_devices()`.
    
    This instantly removes nasty COM object handling (`IPropertyStore`, `IMMDevice`) from your UI or settings services, replacing them with clean `device_info` structs.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 2: Port the Render Path">
    If your app already decodes its own PCM data, simply wrap your decoded frames in a Sonotide render callback. Stop worrying about `IAudioRenderClient::GetBuffer()`.
    
    ```cpp
    class pcm_render_callback final : public sonotide::render_callback {
    public:
        sonotide::result<void> on_render(
            sonotide::audio_buffer_view buffer,
            sonotide::stream_timestamp) override {
            // Copy your already-prepared PCM frames into buffer.bytes
            return sonotide::result<void>::success();
        }
    };
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 3: Consider the Playback Session">
    If you realize your app is manually doing file decoding, tracking playback transport (play/pause), and handling volume all on the main thread — it's time to delete that code. Move entirely to `runtime::open_playback_session()` and let Sonotide handle the orchestration.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 4: Move Capture & Loopback">
    Once your output (render) path is stable, switch your microphone capture and system loopback logic to `runtime::open_capture_stream()` and `runtime::open_loopback_stream()`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 5: Delete the Legacy Code">
    When the new path is stable and your tests are green, brutally delete the old layer built on `IAudioClient`, `IAudioRenderClient`, and `IAudioCaptureClient`. Enjoy the negative line count in your pull request!
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What SHOULD stay in your app?

Even after migrating to Sonotide, your application should still own:
- Decoding (unless you explicitly use `playback_session`).
- Application state and UI.
- User settings persistence (saving the preferred `device_id` to disk).
- Product logic that does not belong in a generic reusable audio layer.
